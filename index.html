Rudolf Reiter


I am a postdoctoral researcher in the fields of controls, optimization, machine learning and robotics in the Robotics and Perception Group in Zürich, Switzerland with Prof. Davide Scaramuzza and, partly, in the Systems Control and Optimization Laboratory in Freiburg, Germany with Prof. Moritz Diehl.
During my Ph.D. with the topic "Optimization-Based Motion Planning and Obstacle Avoidance for Autonomous Driving and Racing", I focused on numerical and combinatorial optimization for autonomous driving, supported by machine learning algorithms to relieve the challenging computational burden. The thesis was written between 2021 and 2024 at the University of Freiburg, Germany, and supervised by Prof. Dr. Moritz Diehl. The Ph.D. was part of the prestigious Marie-Skłodowska Curie Innovative Training Network for "embedded learning and optimization for the next generation of industrial systems". In the course of my doctoral research, I stayed at IMT Lucca with Prof. Dr. Alberto Bemporad, at the Mitsubishi Electric Research Laboratories in Cambridge, MA, US, and at ETH Zürich with Prof. Dr. Melanie Zeilinger to collaborate on research projects.
Before starting my doctoral research, I studied electrical engineering at the Graz University of Technology, Austria, and worked in industry for several years. In my Master's program, I focused on control systems and obtained the Master's degree in 2016. From 2016 to 2018, I worked as a control systems specialist at Anton Paar GmbH and from 2018 to 2021 as an applied scientist for the Virtual Vehicle Research Center in Graz, Austria. Moreover, I was a member of the Autonomous Racing Graz team between 2021 and 2024, where we competed in real-world autonomous racing competitions.
My research focuses on learning- and optimization-based motion planning and control for autonomous systems. I am particularly interested model-based RL, such as approaches that embed predictive planners into RL policies.
